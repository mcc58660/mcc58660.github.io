Staying Organized

I think one of the keys to being successful in college to keep track of due dates, homework, quizzes, and test. With so many classes and having them on different days, I find it impossible to just “remember” when things are due and when exams are. Teachers especially do not give reminders when it comes to dues dates, as they expect their students to know upcoming dates because they give out a syllabus.

The best way for me to keep track of all of my classes is to a planner. A planner works really well for me because I can see months in advance, as well as the week that I have ahead of me. In addition, keeping a running list of tasks that I need to complete on my computer is really helpful because I am constantly on my computer doing school work. When I have my “To Do” list in my line of sight, I am constantly reminded to finish those tasks.

This is how I plan my week and what a typical weekly spread of activities and school work looks like for me.

Helpful Tricks to Stay Organized

  1. Use colored pens or highlighters to categorize important events.
  2. Commit to a specific day to gather the week’s tasks and information.
  3. Remember to look ahead, because some tests take days to study the information.
  4. At the beginning of the semester, look at the syllabus and write down important upcoming dates such as

  • Major Exams
  • Final Exam Dates
  • Upcoming events and activities
  • The professor's office hours
